Common questions

What American buyers usually ask first.

Do I need a finished CAD drawing?

No. A physical sample, images, measurements or a clear explanation can begin the review. Formal technical information will be developed before tooling approval.

Can Tin Tech make low-volume custom products?

Some products can begin with flexible runs, while others require higher quantities because of tooling, resin, decoration or packaging economics. The review will identify the practical entry point.

Who owns the mold?

Mold ownership, storage, maintenance and exclusivity should be written into each proposal before payment. Dedicated client-owned tooling can be structured where appropriate.

Can you deliver directly in the United States?

Tin Tech can coordinate export freight, New York warehousing and onward delivery options. Final scope and cost depend on shipment size, Incoterms, destination and fulfillment needs.

Are all products automatically certified for U.S. sale?

No. Requirements depend on the product, material, claims and intended use. Tin Tech will distinguish standard manufacturing documentation from testing or certification that must be completed for a particular project.
